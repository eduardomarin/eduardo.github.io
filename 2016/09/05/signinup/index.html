Sign In/Up and Global Navigation

This is one of the first large projects I chose to work on at Priceline. Consistency across paths, especially in these two areas (sign in/up and navigation), has been something Priceline struggles with and something that designers hardly pick up for its level of complexity.

Global Navigation - Information (links) Analysis


I began by analyzing the information in the global navigation, as it was already apparent that we needed to standardize the width of the container and enhance visuals overall. I surveyed all the different global navigation “flavors”, and then proceeded to outline what I thought was important and what was not.



Global Navigation - Wireframes


The wireframes I made covered visual changes as well as ideas for potential features. In conceptualizing these ideas I was aware that most of these changes wouldn’t be implemented any time soon. Our team was only 2 people after all: a developer and myself.


Global Navigation - Ideas


Some ideas didn’t make it to polished wireframes, but served a good purpose in explaining these concepts to other teams.


Global Navigation - V1


The version we launched didn’t include any new features or information revamp; it was purely visual.


Sign in / Sign up


I analyzed the current pages we used to sign in and sign up users and took notes on the components we needed to include. The idea in this revamp was to keep changes in functionality to a minimum and focus more on creating pages and modals that would be consistent and accessible from all parts of the site.


Sign in / Sign up - Iterations


Much of the iterations happened more so in wireframes and prototypes. I contributed to the development of a lot of the components.



Sign in / Sign up - Validation Components


We went from laying a list of error messages on top of the form fields (on the old experience) to signaling the errors individually on each form field.



Sign in / Sign up - Conclusion


Creating consistency is tough, especially when resources are low. In the short time I worked on this, I managed to push a more visually sound navigation and a sign/up modal. As a team, we managed to create enough momentum for the creation of a new team: The Global Components team.
