Sole Designer and Front-end dev

Looken

Looken was the smallest startup I’ve ever joined. The team was 6 people, most of them engineers including the CEO; I was the only designer and front-end developer. At the time we were trying to build a platform that accrued deals (like Groupon) from various providers. The main difference was in that we’d cover not only products but services and events as well

First Project - GPS Disabled


I worked on many projects in parallel to this one. It wasn’t too often that I made wireframes; I mostly drafted things, mocked them up and jumped straight to code. I didn’t need to spec things out after all. These are one of the few explorations I was tasked with for the input of a user’s location if GPS is somehow disabled.



Wireframe to Code


I sketched out things first, created wireframes and jumped straight to seeing how we could build this in Angular. Thinking simultaneously about performance and usability made things a bit more difficult but effective somehow.




Responsive Modules


A lot of the visual iterations happened in CSS. Making something responsive after all is much easier when the canvas you are working with is dynamic.



Conclusion


My experience at Looken was invaluable. Working with so few people, so many ideas and immense amounts of autonomy, so much passion just to “make it happen.” I left Looken knowing the kind of designer I wanted to become and what I wanted to impact.
