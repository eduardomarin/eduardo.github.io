EXPRESS DEALS

@Priceline Flights
Express Deals are affordable (super cheap) last minute flights with a little catch: hidden information .

The Express Deals Team emerged just as I switched into the Flights crew. This was an incredible experience, because it was the first project I got to design from scratch. From just a cool inventory of flight deals to being the simplest way for a flexible customer to arrive at a place for far less than any retail price. Express Deals is now a huge contributor in Flights and plans on adding multiple features to give the user more control and lessen the uncertainty when choosing an Express Deal flight.

The struggles were in figuring out how much information to show at different points with the limited amount of info we could show....

WEEKNDER APP

@Weeknder
Weeknder is a web app we created to cater to the needs of our spontaneous users.

I started working on Weeknder at the same time I started on Express Deals. Both use the same inventory but the approaches are slightly different. One is search-based, the other (Weeknder) is more exploratory and therefore relies more on browsing. The difficulties have been many…

Designing and implementing a fully responsive web app in less than a month and collaborating with a new team was perhaps the biggest difficulty in the beginning (fortunately we are only 4 people). Our main objective was releasing an MVP that was aesthetic, functional and optimized for performance; I believe we have done that and now we are iterating to make discovery even better.

SEAT MAPS

@Commercialization Priceline
I recently started working on revamping the entire Seat Maps experience.

Our current experience on desktop is slightly buggy and still uses some antiquated design patterns (cough cough jpgs for seat representation). We currently don’t have a mobile seat maps experience; therefore we need to make this a responsive (maybe adaptive) web app to cover both mobile devices and desktop.

Naturally the struggle lies in creating the flow and figuring out the micro interactions that go into selecting seats. The details as well such as the change in seat sizes based upon the screen size and other design minutiae that solidifies our experience altogether.

VISUAL REDESIGN

@littleBits
LittleBits is a platform of easy-to-use electronic building blocks for creating inventions large and small.

I joined littleBits as their third designer for a brief period. I mainly focused on restructuring the navigation and giving certain landing pages a new fresh look. This was an interesting project, as before this I did not think much of my visual design skills, however after collaborating with two fantastic graphic turned UX/UI designers, I manage to learn many things about the aesthetics of a page.

As a freelancer I did not have much insight into how the culture worked and how our designs were brought to life in the hands of our developers. This I considered to be a major challenge, as I like working closely with developers to understand beyond the design challenges: what things are feasible from their perspective and how we can improve the design/integration interaction.

WSJ WHAT'S NEWS APP
