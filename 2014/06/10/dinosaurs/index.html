Wynn Landing Page

Redesigning a room availability tool

Role: UI Designer

Wynn is a resort and casino located in Las Vegas. A few months ago, while browsing through some famous casinos’ websites, I stumbled upon the Wynn’s home page, which abruptly caught my attention for the funky music playing in the background. While I navigated the site I began to take notice of the many similarities across all the major casinos’ websites. Mainly in that most of them were trying to accentuate this room availability search tool by keeping this rather unappealing tool in all pages. I thought there might be a superior way to keep this room availability omnipresent, so I started working on sketches and ended up coming up with something much simpler.

Unclutter the Room Availability Tool

Instead of keeping a hideous tool with all of its fields in all pages, I decided to make a button that serves the same purpose but effectively reduces the space consumed by the latter.

Focus on what you are known for

The Wynn has incredible things to offer, ranging from nightclubs to shows to spas. The difference, I thought, must be made through simplicity in the UI highlighting the great things the Wynn has to offer. It’s not enough to see a picture of the casino and some music in the background. Enhancing what the user wants to see creates a greater experience.

Conclusion

My take on redesigning the Wynn’s homepage was simple. I merely wanted to reduce the space occupied by the room availability tool and concentrate on conveying the great things the Wynn has to offer. Although I comprehend that Wynn, among with other major casinos, are no Google or Facebook, I also think an aesthetic edge and a usable interface can greatly influence a casino’s success.
